### Question 5 ✅ A) With each monthly deposit, the amount increased by \$25

$$f(t) = 100 + 25t$$

This is a linear function in slope-intercept form where:
- $100$ is the **initial** amount (before any deposits, $t=0$)
- $25$ is the **rate of change** — the amount added per deposit

Therefore, 25 represents a **$\$25$ increase per monthly deposit**.

### Question 9 ✅ B) 72°

> [!tip] Topic & Method

Triangles $PQR$ and $STU$ are similar with $P \leftrightarrow S$.

In right triangle $PQR$:
$$\angle P + \angle Q + \angle R = 180°$$
$$\angle P + 18° + 90° = 180°$$

Since $P$ corresponds to $S$, we have $\angle S = \angle P = \mathbf{72°}$.

### Question 14 ✅ 80

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **System of Linear Equations** · Subtract the equations to eliminate $y$, solve for $x$, then back-substitute.

$$\begin{cases} 24x + y = 48 \\ 6x + y = 72 \end{cases}$$

Subtract the second equation from the first:

$$(24x + y) - (6x + y) = 48 - 72$$

$$18x = -24 \implies x = -\frac{4}{3}$$

Substitute into $6x + y = 72$:

$$6\!\left(-\frac{4}{3}\right) + y = 72$$

$$-8 + y = 72 \implies y = \mathbf{80}$$

**Verification:** $24\!\left(-\tfrac{4}{3}\right) + 80 = -32 + 80 = 48$ ✓

### Question 21 ✅ $\dfrac{361}{8}$

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Simplifying Radical/Exponent Expressions** · Rewrite radicals as fractional exponents, then apply exponent rules to combine.

$$6\sqrt[5]{3^5 x^{45}} \cdot \sqrt[8]{2^8 x}$$

Rewrite using fractional exponents:

$$= 6 \cdot \left(3^5 x^{45}\right)^{1/5} \cdot \left(2^8 x\right)^{1/8}$$

$$= 6 \cdot 3^{5/5} \cdot x^{45/5} \cdot 2^{8/8} \cdot x^{1/8}$$

$$= 6 \cdot 3 \cdot x^9 \cdot 2 \cdot x^{1/8}$$

$$= 36 \cdot x^{9 + 1/8}$$

$$= 36 \cdot x^{73/8}$$

So $a = 36$ and $b = \dfrac{73}{8}$.

$$a + b = 36 + \frac{73}{8} = \frac{288}{8} + \frac{73}{8} = \boxed{\dfrac{361}{8}}$$

### Question 23 ✅ D) $\dfrac{45}{k}$

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Factoring Polynomials** · Expand the factored form to match coefficients, then identify which ratio is guaranteed to be an integer.

$$4x^2 + bx - 45 = (hx + k)(x + j)$$

Expanding the right side:

$$= hx^2 + (hj + k)x + kj$$

Matching coefficients:
- $h = 4$
- $kj = -45$
- $b = hj + k$

Since $k$ and $j$ are **integers** with $kj = -45$, it follows that $k$ is an integer divisor of $-45$, meaning:

$$j = \frac{-45}{k} \implies \frac{45}{k} = -j$$

Because $j$ is an integer, $\dfrac{45}{k}$ is **always an integer**.

The other options ($b/h$, $b/k$) depend on specific values of $h$, $j$, $k$ and are not guaranteed to be integers. $45/h = 45/4$, which is not an integer.

### Question 24 ✅ C) 6

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **System of Equations / Discriminant** · Substitute one equation into the other to get a quadratic, then set the discriminant to zero for exactly one intersection.

Substituting $y = 3x + a$ into $y = 2x^2 - 21x + 64$:

$$3x + a = 2x^2 - 21x + 64$$

$$2x^2 - 24x + (64 - a) = 0$$

For **exactly one solution**, the discriminant must equal zero:

$$\Delta = (-24)^2 - 4(2)(64 - a) = 0$$

$$576 - 8(64 - a) = 0$$

$$576 - 512 + 8a = 0$$

$$8a = -64 \implies a = -8$$

Substitute $a = -8$ back:

$$2x^2 - 24x + 72 = 0$$

$$x^2 - 12x + 36 = 0$$

$$(x - 6)^2 = 0 \implies x = \mathbf{6}$$

$$29\sqrt{2} + 29\sqrt{2} + 58 = 58 + 58\sqrt{2}$$

### Question 26 ✅ D) $-12$

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Parabolas / Vertex Form** · Write the equation in vertex form, recognize that $a+b+c = f(1)$, then determine the sign constraint on $a$.

With vertex $(9, -14)$, the parabola is:

$$y = a(x - 9)^2 - 14$$

Note that $a + b + c = f(1)$ (evaluating the standard form at $x = 1$):

$$f(1) = a(1 - 9)^2 - 14 = 64a - 14$$

Since the parabola intersects the x-axis at **two points** and the vertex is at $y = -14 < 0$, the parabola must **open upward**, so $a > 0$.

Therefore: $f(1) = 64a - 14 > -14$

From the choices, only **D) $-12$** satisfies $f(1) > -14$:

$$64a - 14 = -12 \implies 64a = 2 \implies a = \frac{1}{32} > 0 \checkmark$$

### Question 27 ✅ $a = 5$

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Exponential Functions / System of Equations** · Use the y-intercept condition to find $b$, then use the product condition to find $a$.

$$f(x) = -a^x + b$$

The graph of $y = f(x) - 15$ has a y-intercept at $\left(0, -\dfrac{99}{7}\right)$.

At $x = 0$:

$$f(0) - 15 = -\frac{99}{7}$$

$$f(0) = -\frac{99}{7} + 15 = -\frac{99}{7} + \frac{105}{7} = \frac{6}{7}$$

Evaluate $f(0)$ directly:

$$f(0) = -a^0 + b = -1 + b$$

So:

$$-1 + b = \frac{6}{7} \implies b = 1 + \frac{6}{7} = \frac{13}{7}$$

Use the product condition $ab = \dfrac{65}{7}$:

$$a \cdot \frac{13}{7} = \frac{65}{7}$$

$$a = \frac{65}{7} \div \frac{13}{7} = \frac{65}{13} = \mathbf{5}$$
