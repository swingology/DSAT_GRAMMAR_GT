## Questions 1–4 | Vocabulary in Context

**Q1 — Answer: B) collected**
OSIRIS-REx gathered pieces of the asteroid's surface to bring back to Earth. "Collected" is the most precise word for taking a physical sample. The other options don't logically fit the action described.
> 📌 *Skill: Words in Context — selecting the most precise verb*

---

**Q2 — Answer: A) reflect**
The passage explains that early impact evidence may no longer be present on the Moon's surface, meaning the surface doesn't accurately *represent* or *show* those events. "Reflect" means to show or mirror — the right fit here.
> 📌 *Skill: Words in Context — precision and logical fit*

---

**Q3 — Answer: A) recognizable**
Handedness is *easy to observe* in humans but *less [blank]* in animals — the contrast calls for a word meaning easily seen. "Recognizable" fits; the other options (intriguing, significant, useful) don't logically contrast with "easy to observe."
> 📌 *Skill: Words in Context — using contrast clues*

---

**Q4 — Answer: C) unimportant**
"By no means unimportant" = it IS important to recognize Bosch's influence, confirmed by "indeed, Banisadr himself cites Bosch." The "However" then introduces the counterpoint that Gilgamesh may have had an even greater impact.
> 📌 *Skill: Words in Context — double negatives and logical flow*

## Questions 5–8 | Text Structure & Purpose

**Q5 — Answer: A) To reveal the shop owner's conflicted feelings about the new picture**
The owner openly "fumed" that the picture didn't belong, yet was "secretly proud of his acquisition." Both reactions are presented together, showing internal conflict — the passage's central point.
> 📌 *Skill: Main Purpose — identifying the author's intent*

---

**Q6 — Answer: B) The speaker describes a distinctive sight in nature, then ponders what meaning to attribute to that sight**
The first part vividly describes the cypress tree; the final two lines ask *why* it is black and pointing upward — questioning its meaning. No human behavior is present (eliminates C), and she doesn't question her own assessment (eliminates A).
> 📌 *Skill: Text Structure — two-part poem analysis*

---

**Q7 — Answer: D) The speaker addresses a criticism leveled against him, then announces a grand ambition of his**
Whitman opens by noting the charge against him ("that I seek to destroy institutions"), dismisses it, then announces his goal: to establish "the institution of the dear love of comrades" everywhere.
> 📌 *Skill: Text Structure — rhetorical structure in poetry*

---

**Q8 — Answer: B) It presents a generalization that is exemplified by the discussion of the mimosa trees and *B. terrenus***
The third sentence ("evolutionary links between predators and their prey can persist across centuries and continents") is a broad claim. The rest of the passage is a specific example supporting that claim.
> 📌 *Skill: Sentence Function — how individual sentences support the whole*

## Questions 9–12 | Comprehension & Main Idea

**Q9 — Answer: B) By disputing the idea that developments in social structures have followed a linear progression through distinct stages**
Text 1 describes a fixed sequence (bands → clans → chiefdoms → states). Graeber and Wengrow argue humans have *always* been socially flexible, moving between hierarchy and decentralized systems — directly challenging the linear model.
> 📌 *Skill: Cross-Text Analysis — relating two perspectives*

---

**Q10 — Answer: D) Mary feels very satisfied when she's taking care of the garden**
Mary works steadily, "becoming more pleased with her work every hour instead of tiring of it" — she finds it like "fascinating play." This points to deep satisfaction, not boredom, avoidance, or a strategic purpose.
> 📌 *Skill: Main Idea — synthesizing character detail*

---

**Q11 — Answer: C) It requires proper nourishment in order to thrive**
The poem directly parallels the mind to a flower: both flourish with "sweet dew" (nourishment) and languish without it. The poem is about the necessity of care — not time, weather, or perseverance.
> 📌 *Skill: Literal Comprehension — identifying an explicit analogy*

---

**Q12 — Answer: D) Buck holds Thornton in higher regard than any other person**
Buck is cold to strangers, tolerates Thornton's partners only because of their connection to Thornton, and "Thornton alone held [Buck]." Every detail points to Thornton's uniquely elevated status.
> 📌 *Skill: Main Idea — identifying the central claim of a passage*

## Questions 13–17 | Data & Evidence

**Q13 — Answer: D) Pennsylvania had more than 1,200 organic farms**
The bar graph shows Pennsylvania's bar clearly exceeds the 1,200 mark. The other options contain inaccuracies: Iowa is well below 1,200, New York is above 800, and Washington reads ~600–700 (arguably accurate but less clearly supported than D).
> 📌 *Skill: Using Data — reading bar charts accurately*

---

**Q14 — Answer: B) Over several generations, the sound made by migratory male feathers grows progressively higher pitched**
The hypothesis is that female preference for certain sounds will drive *further genetic and anatomical divergence over time*. Only B shows the predicted long-term trend — progressive change across generations.
> 📌 *Skill: Evaluating Evidence — identifying support for a hypothesis*

---

**Q15 — Answer: C) iron from HTC dust is 90%**
The text introduces AST iron at 28% as the "slower" baseline and needs a contrasting example from a *faster* dust source. Iron from HTC (90%) provides a sharp, same-element contrast that directly illustrates the stated principle.
> 📌 *Skill: Using Data — matching table data to a specific comparison*

---

**Q16 — Answer: A) "…we had a hard time sharing credit and responsibility for our work, the collective didn't last"**
The journalist's claim is that collaboration is *difficult for artists used to sole control*. Only A directly shows that difficulty causing a real breakdown. B, C, and D all describe positive or neutral experiences with collaboration.
> 📌 *Skill: Illustrating a Claim — selecting a quotation that matches an argument*

---

**Q17 — Answer: A) broccoli grown in soil with fungi had a slightly higher average mass than broccoli in fungi-killed soil (7.5 vs. 7.0 g)**
The student was *surprised* — meaning the result defied expectations. Broccoli is a *non-host* species that shouldn't benefit from fungi, yet it still grew slightly larger with fungi present. That's the unexpected finding.
> 📌 *Skill: Using Data — identifying the "surprising" result given experimental context*

## Questions 19–26 | Standard English Conventions (Grammar)

**Q19 — Answer: A) people's stories**
"People's" is the correct possessive form of the plural noun *people*. Ghostwriters write *other people's stories* — apostrophe after "people" shows ownership.
> 📌 *Skill: Possessives vs. Plurals*

---

**Q20 — Answer: D) survives**
The sentence describes a general, ongoing biological behavior (the wood frog's survival strategy during cold seasons). Present tense ("survives") is appropriate for scientific fact.
> 📌 *Skill: Verb Tense — present tense for habitual/ongoing facts*

---

**Q21 — Answer: D) ran—fast. During**
"She ran—fast" is a complete sentence. "During the 1960 Summer Olympics… she won both dashes" is then a new sentence with full detail. The period creates a clean break. Other options create run-on sentences or awkward punctuation.
> 📌 *Skill: Punctuation — em dashes and sentence boundaries*

---

**Q22 — Answer: D) is**
The subject is the singular noun "triangle" (not "paintings"), so the verb must be singular: "the triangle… **is** among the few defined figures."
> 📌 *Skill: Subject-Verb Agreement — identifying the true subject*

---

**Q23 — Answer: B) sampler;**
Two independent clauses are joined: "…craft a sampler" and "in 2014, she arranged…". A semicolon correctly joins them without a conjunction.
> 📌 *Skill: Semicolons — joining independent clauses*

---

**Q24 — Answer: C) Julian's 1935 synthesis of the alkaloid physostigmine**
The introductory phrase "Named in 1999 as one of the greatest achievements" must modify the *thing that was named* — the synthesis itself, not Julian or the alkaloid alone. Only C makes the synthesis the grammatical subject.
> 📌 *Skill: Dangling Modifiers — matching an introductory phrase to the correct noun*

---

**Q25 — Answer: B) species, both native and nonnative;**
The sentence lists three parallel activities separated by semicolons: "growing diverse plant species, both native and nonnative**;** fostering scientific research**;** and educating the public." The comma after "nonnative" is an interrupting nonessential phrase; the semicolon separates the list items.
> 📌 *Skill: Semicolons in Lists — when list items contain internal commas*

---

**Q26 — Answer: B) single-handedly; however,**
"Okinaka doesn't make decisions single-handedly" is a complete independent clause. "However, all historical designations must be approved…" is also independent. A semicolon before *however* and a comma after it is the standard convention.
> 📌 *Skill: Conjunctive Adverbs — punctuating "however" between two clauses*

## Questions 27–30 | Transitions

**Q27 — Answer: C) Finally,**
After 15 years of repeated failed submissions, "Finally" captures the sense of long-awaited resolution. "Instead" implies a change of direction; "Likewise" and "Additionally" don't convey the culminating moment.
> 📌 *Skill: Transitions — expressing sequence and resolution*

---

**Q28 — Answer: D) However,**
The passage shifts from the long-held belief (Mauna Loa is largest) to a contradicting 2020 finding (Pūhāhonu is larger). "However" signals the contrasting information.
> 📌 *Skill: Transitions — contrast words*

---

**Q29 — Answer: A) In addition,**
Coleridge-Taylor emphasized his mixed-race ancestry in two ways: calling himself "Anglo-African" AND incorporating African music. "In addition" introduces a second example of the same behavior. "However" would imply contrast, which isn't present.
> 📌 *Skill: Transitions — additive vs. contrasting transitions*

---

**Q30 — Answer: A) Therefore,**
The researchers tasted the dish and found it mild and calming → *therefore* they theorized the name "unwinding" referred to its relaxing function. The second clause is a logical conclusion drawn from the first.
> 📌 *Skill: Transitions — cause-and-effect / conclusion words*

## Questions 31–33 | Rhetorical Synthesis (Notes to Text)

**Q31 — Answer: D) To produce carbon dioxide within a liquid batter, baking soda needs to be mixed with an acidic ingredient, whereas baking powder does not**
The goal is to *emphasize a difference* between the two agents. Only D directly contrasts them on the same criterion (needing an acid). A and B describe similarities; C is about composition, not function.
> 📌 *Skill: Rhetorical Synthesis — matching the stated goal to relevant information*

---

**Q32 — Answer: D) In *Unwoven Light*, a 2013 work by Korean American artist Soo Sunny Park, light formed colorful prisms as it passed through a fence Park had fitted with iridescent tiles**
The goal is to *describe the artwork to someone unfamiliar with Park*. D introduces the artist, names and dates the work, and describes its most notable effect (colorful prisms). Other options omit the visual effect or omit key context.
> 📌 *Skill: Rhetorical Synthesis — audience awareness and completeness*

---

**Q33 — Answer: C) Using a novel digital imaging technique, Tan revealed hundreds of images hidden on the walls of Angkor Wat, a Cambodian temple**
The goal is to *present Tan's research to someone unfamiliar with Angkor Wat*. C introduces what Angkor Wat is ("a Cambodian temple"), focuses on Tan's key finding, and explains the technique's role — all in one sentence. D omits Tan's research; A and B are incomplete.
> 📌 *Skill: Rhetorical Synthesis — prioritizing information for a specific audience*
