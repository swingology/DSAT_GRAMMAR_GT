### Question 18 | Standard English Conventions (Misplaced Modifier)

In 2016, engineer Vanessa Galvez oversaw the installation of 164 bioswales, vegetated channels designed to absorb and divert stormwater, along the streets of Queens, New York. By reducing the runoff flowing into city sewers, ______.

- A) the mitigation of both street flooding and the resulting pollution of nearby waterways has been achieved by bioswales.
- **B) the bioswales have mitigated both street flooding and the resulting pollution of nearby waterways. ✅**
- C) the bioswales' mitigation of both street flooding and the resulting pollution of nearby waterways has been achieved.
- D) both street flooding and the resulting pollution of nearby waterways have been mitigated by bioswales.

> **Answer: B** — The introductory phrase "By reducing the runoff flowing into city sewers" must be followed by the subject doing the reducing — the bioswales. Only B places "the bioswales" as the subject of the main clause, correcting the misplaced modifier.

### Question 19 | Standard English Conventions (Punctuation)

From afar, African American fiber artist Bisa Butler's portraits look like paintings, their depictions of human faces, bodies, and clothing so intricate that it seems only a fine brush could have rendered them. When viewed up close, however, the portraits reveal themselves to be ______ stitching barely visible among the thousands of pieces of printed, microcut fabric.

- A) quilts, and the
- **B) quilts, the ✅**
- C) quilts; the
- D) quilts. The

> **Answer: B** — "the portraits reveal themselves to be quilts" is an independent clause, followed by "the stitching barely visible among the thousands of pieces of printed, microcut fabric," which is an absolute phrase (not an independent clause). A comma correctly separates the clause from the absolute phrase. A semicolon (C) or period (D) would require another independent clause.

### Question 20 | Standard English Conventions (Comparisons)

Compared to that of alumina glass, ______ silica glass atoms are so far apart that they are unable to re-form bonds after being separated.

- A) silica glass is at a significant disadvantage due to its more dispersed atomic arrangement;
- B) silica glass has a more dispersed atomic arrangement, resulting in a significant disadvantage;
- C) a significant disadvantage of silica glass is that its atomic arrangement is more dispersed;
- **D) silica glass's atomic arrangement is more dispersed, resulting in a significant disadvantage; ✅**

> **Answer: D** — The sentence compares "that of alumina glass" (i.e., the atomic arrangement of alumina glass) to something equivalent. Only D correctly compares the atomic arrangement of alumina glass to the atomic arrangement of silica glass ("silica glass's atomic arrangement"), maintaining a logical parallel comparison.

### Question 21 | Standard English Conventions (Verb Form)

In the historic novel *The Surrender Tree*, Cuban American author Margarita Engle ______ poetry rather than prose to tell the story of Cuba's wars of independence through the perspectives of real people from that era.

- **A) uses ✅**
- B) is using
- C) used
- D) has been using

> **Answer: A** — When discussing the content or techniques of a literary work, the literary present tense (simple present) is standard. "Engle uses" is the correct form.

### Question 22 | Standard English Conventions (Semicolons / Transitions)

Sociologist Alton Okinaka sits on a review board tasked with adding new sites to the Hawaiʻi Register of Historic Places, which includes hundreds of culturally significant locations. Okinaka doesn't make such decisions single-handedly; ______ all historical designations must be approved by a group of nine other experts from the fields of architecture, archeology, history, and Hawaiian culture.

- **A) however, ✅**
- B) for instance,
- C) therefore,
- D) in addition,

> **Answer: A** — Both clauses are independent (semicolon is correct). The second clause ("all designations must be approved by a group") contrasts with "Okinaka doesn't make decisions single-handedly" by explaining the broader system — the sentence order is reversed from how it would normally be written, making "however" the right contrast transition. The difficulty is that the sentence is structured with the specific example first and the general rule second.

### Question 24 | Transitions (Logical Completion)

Researchers Helena Lucia and Marco report that while mathematicians may have traditionally worked alone, evidence points to a shift in the opposite direction: ______ mathematicians are choosing to collaborate with their peers, a trend that may be accelerating.

- **A) increasingly, ✅**
- B) however,
- C) in fact,
- D) for example,

> **Answer: A** — The passage states mathematicians "traditionally worked alone" but are now shifting toward collaboration. The word "increasingly" indicates that this collaborative trend is growing over time, which is precisely what the sentence conveys ("a trend that may be accelerating").

### Question 25 | Transitions (Logical Completion)

When soil is contaminated by toxic metals, it can be removed from the ground and disposed of in a landfill. ______ contaminated soil can be detoxified via phytoremediation: plants that can withstand high concentrations of metals absorb the pollutants and store them in their shoots, which are then cut off and safely disposed of, preserving the health of the soil.

- A) For example,
- B) In particular,
- **C) Alternatively, ✅**
- D) Similarly,

> **Answer: C** — The first sentence describes one method (removal/landfill); the second sentence describes a completely different method (phytoremediation). "Alternatively" is the only transition that signals a different option or approach, which is what the relationship between the sentences calls for.

### Question 26 | Rhetorical Synthesis (Effective Use of Information)

A student is writing a report on the decline of apple variety in the United States and wants to emphasize the decline in unique apple varieties **and** specify why this decline occurred. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information to accomplish this goal?

*(Passage context: In the late 1890s, there were 14,000 unique apple varieties. The rise of industrial agriculture in the mid-1900s narrowed commercially grown crops; thousands of varieties were lost. Today only 15 remain.)*

- A) The Lost Apple Project works to recover forgotten apple varieties that were once grown across the United States.
- **B) Since the rise of industrial agriculture in the mid-1900s narrowed the range of commercially grown crops, many unique apple varieties were lost; today only 15 of the 14,000 varieties recorded in the late 1890s remain. ✅**
- C) U.S. farmers have mainly grown the same apple varieties for decades, resulting in the loss of thousands of unique types that once existed.
- D) The number of crops selected for mass cultivation rose to prominence in the mid-1900s, contributing to a broader loss of agricultural diversity.

> **Answer: B** — Only B both emphasizes the magnitude of the decline (14,000 → 15) AND specifies the cause (rise of industrial agriculture in the mid-1900s). A doesn't mention the decline or cause. C states a cause but lacks the specific numbers showing decline. D is too broad (talks about crops in general, not apples specifically).

### Question 27 | Rhetorical Synthesis (Effective Use of Information)

- **A) Catherine Halverson's *Far Away Women in the Atlantic Monthly* discusses female authors whose autobiographical writing appeared in the magazine in the early 1900s. ✅**
- B) A magazine called *The Atlantic Monthly*, referred to in Catherine Halverson's book, was first published in 1857 focused on literature and culture.
- C) *Far Away Women in the Atlantic Monthly* features contributors to *The Atlantic Monthly*, first published as a magazine focusing on literature and culture.
- D) An author discussed in Catherine Halverson's book published autobiographical writing in a magazine in the early 1900s.

> **Answer: A** — The task is to introduce Halverson's *book* to an audience already *familiar* with The Atlantic Monthly. A correctly names the book, connects it directly to the magazine the audience already knows, and provides meaningful content
