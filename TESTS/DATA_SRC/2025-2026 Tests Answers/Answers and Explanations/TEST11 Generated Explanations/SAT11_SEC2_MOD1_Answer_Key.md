### Question 1 — Isosceles Triangle Angles

**Correct Answer: D (x = 24)**
**Topic:** Geometry — Triangle Properties
**Skill:** Isosceles triangle base angles

The sum of interior angles in any triangle is $180°$. Given $\angle Q = 132°$ and $\angle P = 24°$:

$$x + 132 + 24 = 180 \implies x = 180 - 156 = 24$$

**Alternate approach:** Since $PQ = QR$, triangle $PQR$ is isosceles, so the base angles $\angle P$ and $\angle R$ are congruent. Therefore $x = 24°$.

> [!tip] Strategy: Isosceles Triangle Shortcut
> When a triangle is labeled as isosceles (equal sides marked), the **base angles are always equal**. Identify which two angles are the base angles to avoid unnecessary calculation.

> [!danger] Trap: Angle Q ≠ Base Angle
> The equal sides are $PQ$ and $QR$, so vertex $Q$ is at the apex — meaning $\angle P$ and $\angle R$ are the base angles, NOT $\angle Q$ and another angle.

### Question 2 — Linear Equation

**Correct Answer: A**
**Topic:** Algebra — Linear Equations
**Skill:** Solving and recognizing equivalent equations

Given $4x + 1 = 33$, subtract 1 from both sides:
$$4x = 32$$
The equation $4x = 32$ has the same solution as the original.

> [!tip] Strategy: Equivalent Equations
> An equation is "equivalent" to another if performing the same operation on both sides. Simply subtracting a constant from both sides gives you an equivalent equation with the same solution.

> [!danger] Trap: Don't Solve All the Way
> The question asks which equation has the **same solution**, not what the solution is. Recognize that subtracting 1 from both sides of $4x+1=33$ gives $4x=32$ — that's all you need.

### Question 3 — Linear Function Slope-Intercept

**Correct Answer: C**
**Topic:** Algebra — Linear Functions
**Skill:** Writing equations in slope-intercept form

A linear function $f(x) = mx + b$ where $m$ = slope and $(0, b)$ = y-intercept.
- Slope $m = 7$
- Graph passes through $(0, 5)$, so $b = 5$

$$f(x) = 7x + 5$$

> [!tip] Strategy: Slope-Intercept Form
> Always identify slope ($m$) and y-intercept ($b$) from the problem, then plug directly into $f(x) = mx + b$.

> [!danger] Trap: Slope ≠ Intercept
> Don't confuse which value is the slope and which is the y-intercept. The slope is the **rate of change** (multiplied by $x$), and the y-intercept is where the line **crosses the y-axis** (constant term).

### Question 4 — Quadratic Equation, Positive Solution

**Correct Answer: A (x = 3)**
**Topic:** Advanced Math — Quadratic Equations
**Skill:** Solving quadratics, selecting the positive solution

Solving $8x^2 - 40 = 32$:
1. Add 40: $8x^2 = 72$
2. Divide by 8: $x^2 = 9$
3. Take square root: $x = \pm 3$

The **positive** solution is $x = 3$.

> [!tip] Strategy: Positive Solution Qualifier
> When a quadratic gives $\pm$ answers, read the question carefully. If it asks for the "positive solution," you can eliminate the negative root immediately.

> [!danger] Trap: Forgetting the ± Sign
> $\sqrt{9} = \pm 3$, not just $3$. Always acknowledge both solutions, then apply the constraint given in the problem.

### Question 10 — Proportional Estimation from Sample

**Correct Answer: C (800)**
**Topic:** Problem-Solving & Data Analysis — Statistical Inference
**Skill:** Scaling sample proportion to population

8 out of 300 surveyed customers said they'd be interested in the new plan. Scale to 30,000 customers:

$$\frac{8}{300} \times 30{,}000 = 800$$

> [!tip] Strategy: Set Up a Proportion
> $$\frac{\text{sample favorable}}{\text{sample size}} = \frac{\text{estimated population favorable}}{\text{population size}}$$
> Cross-multiply to solve.

> [!danger] Trap: Reporting the Sample Count
> The answer is **800** (scaled to the full population), not 8 (the raw sample count). Always apply the proportion to the total population.

### Question 11 — Factoring Algebraic Expressions

**Correct Answer: D**
**Topic:** Advanced Math — Factoring
**Skill:** Factoring out the GCF from polynomial terms

Factor $64t^2s^3 - 56t^3s$:
- GCF of coefficients: $\gcd(64, 56) = 4 \times 2 = 8$... wait, let me re-check. The GCF is $4t^2s$
- $64t^2s^3 - 56t^3s = 4t^2s(16s^2 - 14t)$

> [!tip] Strategy: Find the GCF of All Terms
> For each term, find the GCF of: (1) the coefficients, (2) the $t$ powers, (3) the $s$ powers. Factor them all out together.

> [!danger] Trap: Incorrect Exponent After Factoring
> After factoring out $t^2$, the first term's $t^2$ cancels (leaving 1), while the second term's $t^3$ becomes $t$. Track exponents carefully.

### Question 14 — Exponential Growth (Gridded)

**Correct Answer: 15,000**
**Topic:** Advanced Math — Exponential Functions
**Skill:** Setting up and solving exponential growth equations

![[assets/q14_population_growth.png]]

The exponential model for population doubling every 75 years:
$$y = a \cdot 2^{x/75}$$

Given: In 1959 ($x = 300$ years after 1659), $y = 240{,}000$:
$$240{,}000 = a \cdot 2^{300/75} = a \cdot 2^4 = 16a$$
$$a = \frac{240{,}000}{16} = 15{,}000$$

The population in 1659 (the starting year) was **15,000**.

> [!tip] Strategy: Build the Exponential Model
> For doubling: $y = a \cdot 2^{x/k}$ where $k$ = doubling period. Find the exponent from elapsed time, then back-calculate $a$.

> [!danger] Trap: Confusing Starting Year
> $x$ represents years **after** 1659, so 1959 corresponds to $x = 300$. Don't use $x = 1959$.

### Question 15 — Slope of Line of Best Fit

**Correct Answer: A (0.6)**
**Topic:** Problem-Solving & Data Analysis — Scatterplots
**Skill:** Estimating slope from two points on a line

![[assets/q15_line_of_best_fit.png]]

Reading two points from the line of best fit: approximately $(0, 3.2)$ and $(2, 4.4)$:

$$\text{slope} = \frac{4.4 - 3.2}{2 - 0} = \frac{1.2}{2} = 0.6$$

> [!tip] Strategy: Pick Two Clear Points on the Line
> Don't use data points — use two points that clearly lie on the **line of best fit**. Choose points where the line crosses grid intersections for accuracy.

> The slope of the line of best fit ≠ slope between two data dots. Always read off the line itself.

### Question 16 — Number of Solutions (Quadratic)

**Correct Answer: A**
**Topic:** Advanced Math — Quadratic Equations
**Skill:** Determining the number of solutions using discriminant/form analysis

$(x + 15)^2 = 0$ → Taking square root: $x + 15 = 0$ → $x = -15$

This equation has **exactly one** distinct real solution.

> [!tip] Strategy: Recognize Perfect Square Forms
> $(x + c)^2 = 0$ always has **exactly one** solution: $x = -c$. The square makes both roots equal.

> [!danger] Trap: Confusing One Repeated Root with Two Roots
> $(x+15)^2 = 0$ technically factors as $(x+15)(x+15) = 0$, giving two equal roots — but these count as **one distinct** solution.

### Question 18 — System of Equations (Graphical)

**Skill:** Matching equations to graphs via intercepts

For $x + y = 53$: x-intercept $(53, 0)$, y-intercept $(0, 53)$
For $11x + 18y = 730$: x-intercept $\approx (66, 0)$, y-intercept $\approx (0, 41)$

### Question 19 — Trigonometry: Right Triangle

**Correct Answer: D ($QS = \dfrac{18}{\sin Q}$)**
**Topic:** Geometry & Trigonometry — Right Triangle Trig
**Skill:** Applying $\sin = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$

![[assets/q19_right_triangle.png]]

In right triangle $QRS$ with $RS = 18$:
$$\sin Q = \frac{RS}{QS} = \frac{18}{QS}$$
$$QS \cdot \sin Q = 18$$
$$QS = \frac{18}{\sin Q}$$

> [!tip] Strategy: SOH-CAH-TOA from the Given Angle
> From angle $Q$: the **opposite** side is $RS = 18$, and the **hypotenuse** is $QS$ (the longest side). Set up $\sin Q = \frac{opp}{hyp}$ and solve for the unknown.

> [!danger] Trap: Using the Wrong Side as Hypotenuse
> The hypotenuse is always opposite the right angle. Here, the right angle is at $R$, so $QS$ (across from it) is the hypotenuse.

### Question 20 — Circle Equation Constant (Gridded)

**Correct Answer: 100**
**Topic:** Geometry — Circles
**Skill:** Using the standard form of a circle equation

Circle A: $(x+5)^2 + (y-5)^2 = 25$ → center $(-5, 5)$, radius $= 5$

Circle B has the same center and **twice the radius** → radius $= 10$:

$$(x+5)^2 + (y-5)^2 = 10^2 = 100$$

So $k = \mathbf{100}$.

> [!tip] Strategy: Standard Circle Form
> $(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$ where $(h, k)$ is the center and $r$ is the radius. Doubling the radius means the right-hand side becomes $(2r)^2 = 4r^2$.

> [!danger] Trap: Doubling $r$ vs. Doubling $r^2$
> Doubling the radius from 5 to 10 means $r^2$ goes from 25 to **100**, not 50. The radius is doubled, not $r^2$.

### Question 21 — Quadratic Formula Constant (Gridded)

**Correct Answer: 29**
**Topic:** Advanced Math — Quadratic Formula
**Skill:** Applying the quadratic formula to identify discriminant

![[assets/q21_quadratic.png]]

For $x^2 + 7x + 5 = 0$ (where $a = 1$, $b = 7$, $c = 5$):

$$x = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{7^2 - 4(1)(5)}}{2(1)} = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{49 - 20}}{2} = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{29}}{2}$$

One solution is $x = \dfrac{-7 + \sqrt{k}}{2}$, so $k = \mathbf{29}$.

> [!tip] Strategy: Memorize the Quadratic Formula
> $$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$
> The value under the radical ($b^2 - 4ac$) is the discriminant. Compute it carefully.

> [!danger] Trap: Order of Operations in Discriminant
> $4ac = 4 \times 1 \times 5 = 20$, not $4 \times 6 = 24$. Don't rush the multiplication.

### Question 22 — Weighted Mean

**Correct Answer: B (90)**
**Topic:** Problem-Solving & Data Analysis — Statistics
**Skill:** Computing weighted mean across two groups

- 240 seals from Muskeget Island: mean length = 88 inches → sum = $240 \times 88 = 21{,}120$
- 120 seals from Sable Island: mean length = 94 inches → sum = $120 \times 94 = 11{,}280$
- Total seals = 360, total sum = 32,400

$$\text{Overall mean} = \frac{32{,}400}{360} = 90 \text{ inches}$$

> [!tip] Strategy: Weighted Mean = Sum of All Values ÷ Total Count
> Never average the two group means directly. Instead, reconstruct each group's total sum (mean × count), add them, then divide by the combined count.

> [!danger] Trap: Simple Average of 88 and 94
> $(88 + 94) / 2 = 91$, which is **wrong** because the two groups are different sizes. The group of 240 has more weight than the group of 120.

### Question 23 — Function Transformation

**Correct Answer: B ($f(x) = -3x + 5$)**
**Topic:** Advanced Math — Function Transformations
**Skill:** Using a point on the graph to find a function constant

Given: graph of $y = f(x) + 4$ passes through $(0, 8)$

Substituting: $8 = f(0) + 4 \implies f(0) = 4$

Each answer is in the form $f(x) = -3x + k$. Substituting $x = 0$, $f(0) = 4$:
$$4 = -3(0) + k \implies k = 5$$
$$f(x) = -3x + 5$$

> [!tip] Strategy: Use the Given Point to Find the Unknown Constant
> If you know the graph of $y = f(x) + c$ passes through a point, you can solve for $f$ at that input first, then use it to find constants in the form of $f$.

> [!danger] Trap: Confusing $y = f(x) + 4$ with $f(x)$ Directly
> The graph shown is $y = f(x) + 4$, **not** $y = f(x)$. The y-value at $(0, 8)$ is 8 for the shifted graph — subtract 4 to get $f(0) = 4$.

### Question 24 — Intersecting Lines, Angle Sums

**Correct Answer: A**
**Topic:** Geometry — Angles Formed by Intersecting Lines
**Skill:** Using supplementary and vertical angle relationships

When two lines intersect, they form two pairs of vertical angles. One angle is $(9x - 140)°$. The possible angle sums of any two angles:
- Two equal angles (vertical): $2(9x - 140)° = (18x - 280)°$
- Two supplementary angles (adjacent): $180°$
- The other pair of vertical angles: $2(180 - (9x-140))° = (–18x + 640)°$

The expression $(-18x + 280)°$ does NOT equal any of these — so it **cannot** be the sum of any two angles.

> [!tip] Strategy: Enumerate All Possible Angle Combinations
> With two lines intersecting, you have 4 angles: two pairs of vertical angles. List the three possible pair sums and see which answer choice matches none of them.

> [!danger] Trap: Forgetting Supplementary Pairs
> Adjacent angles are supplementary (sum to 180°) — this is always a constant, regardless of $x$.

### Question 25 — System of Equations: At Least One Solution

**Correct Answer: C (Both Statement I and Statement II)**
**Topic:** Algebra — Systems of Linear Equations
**Skill:** Determining when a system has at least one solution

Given equation: $2x + 9y = 7$

- **Statement I:** $3x + 13.5y = 10.5$ — multiplying the given by $3/2$ yields exactly this → **same line → infinitely many solutions** ✓
- **Statement II:** $3x - 13.5y = 10.5$ — different slopes, will intersect at exactly one point → **one solution** ✓

Both statements yield a system with at least one solution.

> [!tip] Strategy: Check for Parallel, Same, or Intersecting Lines
> Two equations have: (a) no solution if parallel (same slope, different intercept), (b) one solution if intersecting, (c) infinitely many if identical. Multiply/divide to check.

> [!danger] Trap: Assuming Only One Statement Can Be True
> The question asks which **could** create a valid system — both statements independently satisfy this condition.

### Question 26 — Surface Area of Rectangular Prism

**Correct Answer: B**
**Topic:** Geometry — Surface Area
**Skill:** Computing surface area from given dimensions

Given:
- Base area $= \frac{2}{3} \cdot 8 = \frac{24}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{8}$... The base area $= \frac{2}{3} t \cdot 8 = \frac{2}{3}t \cdot 8$

Let me re-read: base area $= \frac{24}{t} \cdot 8 \cdot t$ — the problem states base area is $\frac{2}{3} \cdot 8$ and length is $\frac{8}{3}$ cm, width = $\frac{9}{t}$ cm, height = 15 cm.

Surface area formula: $2lw + 2lh + 2wh$:
- Two bases: $2 \times \frac{24}{t} = \frac{48}{t}$ cm²
- Two long lateral faces ($\frac{8}{3}$ by 15): $2 \times \frac{8}{3} \times 15 = 80$ cm²
- Two narrow lateral faces ($\frac{9}{t}$ by 15): $2 \times \frac{9}{t} \times 15 = \frac{270}{t}$ cm²

Total: $\frac{48}{t} + 80 + \frac{270}{t} = \frac{318}{t} + 80$ cm²

> [!tip] Strategy: Surface Area = 2(lw + lh + wh)
> For a rectangular prism, you have 3 pairs of faces. Compute the area of each pair and sum them.

> [!danger] Trap: Confusing Surface Area with Volume
> Volume $= l \times w \times h$ (no factor of 2). Surface area uses the 2× formula above.

### Question 27 — Linear Function Constant (Gridded)

**Correct Answer: 3,331**
**Topic:** Algebra — Linear Functions
**Skill:** Solving for a constant in a defined function

Given $f(x) = \frac{1}{7}(x - a) + 433$ and $f(3331) = 433$:

$$433 = \frac{1}{7}(3331 - a) + 433$$
$$0 = \frac{1}{7}(3331 - a)$$
$$3331 - a = 0$$
$$a = 3331$$

> [!tip] Strategy: Substitute and Solve
> When the output value equals the constant term (+433), the rest of the expression must equal zero. Set $\frac{1}{7}(x - a) = 0$ → $x = a$.

> [!info] Real-World Context
> This problem models engine power (bhp) as a function of engine speed (rpm). The constant $a$ represents the engine speed at which the linear model's variable component equals zero.
