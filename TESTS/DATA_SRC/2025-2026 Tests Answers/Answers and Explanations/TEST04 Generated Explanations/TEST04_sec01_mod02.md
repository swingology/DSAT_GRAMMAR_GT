## Questions 1–8 | Vocabulary in Context

**Q1 — Answer: D) predicted**  
The sentence says analysts are “expecting to see continued growth,” so the logical word is **predicted**. The other choices do not fit the context of forecasting future revenue.

**Q2 — Answer: D) overcome**  
The passage describes a difficulty in getting biomolecules through plant cell walls, then says carbon nanotubes may make it possible to solve that difficulty. **Overcome** is the precise word for solving or getting past a problem.

**Q3 — Answer: B) inspecting**  
Particle physicists examine things that are invisible to the naked eye by using sophisticated technology to study subatomic particles. **Inspecting** best matches the idea of closely examining something.

**Q4 — Answer: B) comparable to**  
The bones of “Little Foot” are said to suggest tree-climbing adaptation, so they would need to be **comparable to** the shoulder and clavicle bones of modern apes that climb frequently.

**Q5 — Answer: B) atypical**  
The sentence says that nearly a dozen of Delany’s characters are poets or writers, so Rydra Wong being a poet is **not atypical**. In other words, it is common in Delany’s work.

**Q6 — Answer: B) created**  
Jeyifous made a new series of images for the exhibition, so **created** is the best fit. The passage describes artistic production, not validation, challenge, or restoration.

**Q7 — Answer: A) proponent of**  
Sumner helped expand opportunities for Black students to study psychology and helped found Howard’s psychology department. That makes him a **proponent of** increasing those opportunities.

**Q8 — Answer: C) buttress**  
The sentence is about the factors that allowed a monarch to support or strengthen a claim to the throne. **Buttress** means support or reinforce, which fits perfectly.

## Questions 9–12 | Structure, Purpose, and Evidence

**Q9 — Answer: C) It offers a detail about how female cuckoos carry out the behavior discussed in the text.**  
The underlined sentence explains that female cuckoos lay eggs quickly when the host birds are away looking for food. That is a specific detail about how they perform the behavior described in the passage.

**Q10 — Answer: A) They watched how each cat moved its ears and head.**  
The passage explicitly states that the researchers measured surprise based on the cats’ **ear and head movements**, so choice A directly matches the text.

**Q11 — Answer: A) The choy sum planted in the soil without coffee grounds were significantly taller at the end of the experiment than the choy sum planted in the mixture of soil and coffee grounds.**  
The hypothesis says slightly acidic soil is more beneficial. If the control group without coffee grounds grew significantly taller, that would most directly weaken the hypothesis.

**Q12 — Answer: B) “While we waited she took out a little, gold powder-box with a mirror in the lid, shook the poor little puff as though she loathed it, and dabbed her lovely nose.”**  
The question asks for a quotation that contrasts the girl’s **pleasant appearance** with her **unpleasant attitude**. “Lovely nose” gives the pleasant appearance, while “as though she loathed it” shows the unpleasant attitude.

## Questions 13–18 | Data, Reasoning, and Logical Completion

**Q13 — Answer: D) was substantially lower than uncertainty about trade policy in 2005 and substantially higher than uncertainty about trade policy in 2010.**  
The graph shows that in **2005**, general economic policy uncertainty is much lower than trade policy uncertainty, but in **2010** it is much higher. That clearly illustrates the claim that a general measure may not fully reflect uncertainty in specific policy areas.

**Q14 — Answer: C) On average, participants who watched the panel correctly answered more questions about the issue than those who watched the debate or the single commentator did.**  
Tannen’s hypothesis is that a noncompetitive format with multiple perspectives is more informative than a debate. If the panel viewers answered more questions correctly, that would most strongly support her claim.

**Q15 — Answer: C) says to himself while striking his head, “Beat at this gate that let thy folly in / And thy dear judgement out!”**  
This quotation most directly expresses Lear’s regret. He is condemning his own foolishness and the loss of his judgment.

**Q16 — Answer: A) many theatergoers and readers today are likely to find Shakespeare’s history plays less engaging than the tragedies.**  
The passage says the tragedies still appeal broadly because of universal themes, but the history plays often require detailed knowledge of English history. Therefore, modern audiences are likely to find the history plays less engaging.

**Q17 — Answer: B) some Ancestral Puebloans migrated to the Rio Grande Valley in the late 1200s and carried farming practices with them.**  
If modern Rio Grande Valley turkeys partly descended from Mesa Verde cultivated turkeys, and those shared markers appear only after 1280, the most logical conclusion is that some people migrated from Mesa Verde to the Rio Grande Valley and brought those practices with them.

**Q18 — Answer: D) will find it difficult to identify a group of people who can function as an appropriate control group for their studies.**  
The passage says researchers need a similar non-office-holding group, but they cannot control election outcomes. That makes finding a proper control group difficult.

## Questions 19–26 | Standard English Conventions

**Q19 — Answer: C) stories of the South Asian immigrants**  
The correct phrase is **stories of the South Asian immigrants**. The plural noun **stories** does not need an apostrophe here, and **immigrants** is simply plural, not possessive.

**Q20 — Answer: A) of**  
The standard expression is **themes of healing, self-discovery, and memory**. No extra punctuation is needed.

**Q21 — Answer: B) company that**  
The clause **that specializes in...** is essential to identifying what kind of company it is, so no comma should separate it from **company**.

**Q22 — Answer: C) carbon-13, (13C),**  
The phrase **(13C)** is a parenthetical clarification of **carbon-13**, so it should be set off with commas on both sides.

**Q23 — Answer: D) walls. With**  
The first part is a complete sentence: Tan noticed markings on the temple walls. The next sentence begins **With the help of digital imaging techniques...** A period is needed to avoid a run-on.

**Q24 — Answer: B) forging**  
The sentence describes Charpentier and Doudna re-creating and reprogramming the genetic scissors, thereby **forging** a tool. The participle correctly shows the result of the action.

**Q25 — Answer: B) the bioswales have mitigated both street flooding and the resulting pollution of nearby waterways.**  
The introductory phrase **By reducing the runoff...** must logically modify **the bioswales**, the thing doing the action. Choice B is the clearest and grammatically correct option.

**Q26 — Answer: B) continents: geological**  
The colon is correct because the phrase after it explains or renames the idea before it. The sentence introduces Earth’s continents and then explains the relevant **geological structures called arcs**.

## Questions 27–33 | Transitions and Rhetorical Synthesis

**Q27 — Answer: A) Afterward,**  
The sentence describes what happened following the rocket failure, so **Afterward** is the best transition.

**Q28 — Answer: A) Alternatively,**  
The first sentence gives one method of dealing with contaminated soil, and the next sentence gives another method. **Alternatively** correctly signals an alternative approach.

**Q29 — Answer: C) Designed so calendar dates would occur on the same day of the week each year, the Hanke-Henry calendar supports more predictable scheduling than does the Gregorian calendar.**  
The question asks for an **advantage** of the Hanke-Henry calendar. Predictable scheduling is the clear advantage described in the notes.

**Q30 — Answer: C) The influence theory holds that the principles of the Great Law of Peace, a centuries-old agreement binding six Native nations in the northeastern US, influenced the US Constitution.**  
Because the audience is unfamiliar with the Haudenosaunee Confederacy, the best answer includes both the theory and enough background context to understand it.

**Q31 — Answer: A) At around 9,800°F, which classifies it as a G star, the Sun is hotter than most but not all of the stars within 10 parsecs of it.**  
This choice directly emphasizes the Sun’s heat **relative to nearby stars**: most are cooler, but some are hotter.

**Q32 — Answer: A) Cathryn Halverson’s Faraway Women and the “Atlantic Monthly” discusses female authors whose autobiographies appeared in the magazine in the early 1900s.**  
Since the audience already knows the Atlantic Monthly, the best introduction to the book focuses on the book’s subject rather than background information about the magazine.

**Q33 — Answer: B) Neither of a magnificent frigatebird’s two ways of acquiring food requires the bird to dive into the water.**  
The question asks for a **similarity** between the two methods. Both methods let the bird get food **without diving into the water**.
