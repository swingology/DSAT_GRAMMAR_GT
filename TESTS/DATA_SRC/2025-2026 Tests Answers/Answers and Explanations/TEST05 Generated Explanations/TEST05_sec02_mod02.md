## Question 4
**Answer: B) $2xy(8x^2y + 7)$**

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Topic:** Algebra — Factoring (Greatest Common Factor)
> **Method:** Identify the GCF of all terms, factor it out, and simplify the remaining expression.

$$16x^3y^2 + 14xy$$

The GCF of $16$ and $14$ is $2$; the GCF of $x^3$ and $x$ is $x$; the GCF of $y^2$ and $y$ is $y$. So the GCF $= 2xy$:

$$= 2xy\!\left(\frac{16x^3y^2}{2xy} + \frac{14xy}{2xy}\right) = \boxed{2xy(8x^2y + 7)}$$

**Verify:** $2xy \cdot 8x^2y = 16x^3y^2$ ✓ and $2xy \cdot 7 = 14xy$ ✓

## Question 16
**Answer: A) 116**

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Topic:** Geometry — Triangle Angle Sum
> **Method:** The three angles of a triangle sum to 180°. Find which answer for $\angle S$ leaves a valid positive measure for $\angle T$.

$$\angle R + \angle S + \angle T = 180°$$
$$63° + \angle S + \angle T = 180°$$
$$\angle S + \angle T = 117°$$

For the triangle to be valid, both $\angle S > 0°$ and $\angle T > 0°$, so $\angle S < 117°$.

- **A) 116°:** $\angle T = 1°$ ✓ — valid triangle
- **B) 118°:** $\angle T = -1°$ ✗ — impossible
- **C) 126°:** $\angle T = -9°$ ✗ — impossible
- **D) 180°:** impossible for a single angle in a triangle ✗

Answer: $\angle S = \boxed{116°}$

## Question 17
**Answer: B) $7x^3 + 10$**

> [!tip] Topic & Method

> **Method:** Distribute the negative sign, then combine like terms.

$$(8x^3 + 8) - (x^3 - 2)$$

## Question 21
**Answer: 66**

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Topic:** Geometry — Rectangle Inscribed in a Circle, Pythagorean Theorem
> **Method:** Use the given relationship between the diagonal and shortest side to find the dimensions, then note that the diagonal of the rectangle equals the diameter of the circle.

Let the shortest side $= s$. The diagonal $= 2s$.

By the Pythagorean theorem (with longest side $= \ell$):

$$s^2 + \ell^2 = (2s)^2 = 4s^2$$
$$\ell^2 = 3s^2 \implies \ell = s\sqrt{3}$$

Area of rectangle:
$$s \cdot \ell = s \cdot s\sqrt{3} = s^2\sqrt{3} = 1{,}089\sqrt{3}$$
$$s^2 = 1{,}089 \implies s = 33$$

Diagonal $= 2s = 2(33) = 66$

Since every vertex of the rectangle lies on the circle, the diagonal of the rectangle is the **diameter** of the circle:

$$\text{Diameter} = \boxed{66}$$

## Question 24
**Answer: D) $5x^2 - 14x + 49 = 0$**

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Topic:** Quadratic Equations — Discriminant
> **Method:** Compute $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$. If $\Delta < 0$, there are no real solutions.

For $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, the discriminant is $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$:

| Choice | $a$ | $b$ | $c$ | $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$ | Real solutions? |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|----------------------|-----------------|
| A | $1$ | $14$ | $-49$ | $196 + 196 = 392$ | Yes ✗ |
| B | $1$ | $-14$ | $49$ | $196 - 196 = 0$ | One (repeated) ✗ |
| C | $5$ | $-14$ | $-49$ | $196 + 980 = 1176$ | Yes ✗ |
| **D** | **5** | **−14** | **49** | $196 - 980 = -784$ | **No ✓** |

Since $\Delta < 0$ for choice D, it has **no real solutions**.

## Question 26
**Answer: C) (10, 2)**

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Topic:** Analytic Geometry — Tangent Lines to Circles
> **Method:** The tangent line is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency. Find the radius slope, take the negative reciprocal for the tangent slope, then write the line equation.

**Step 1 — Slope of the radius** from center $(-1, 1)$ to tangent point $(5, -4)$:

$$m_{\text{radius}} = \frac{-4 - 1}{5 - (-1)} = \frac{-5}{6}$$

**Step 2 — Slope of tangent line** (perpendicular):

$$m_t = \frac{6}{5}$$

**Step 3 — Equation of line $t$** through $(5, -4)$:

$$y + 4 = \frac{6}{5}(x - 5) \implies y = \frac{6}{5}x - 10$$

**Step 4 — Test the answer choices:**

- A) $(0,\, \tfrac{6}{5})$: $\tfrac{6}{5}(0) - 10 = -10 \neq \tfrac{6}{5}$ ✗
- B) $(4, 7)$: $\tfrac{6}{5}(4) - 10 = -\tfrac{26}{5} \neq 7$ ✗
- **C) $(10, 2)$:** $\tfrac{6}{5}(10) - 10 = 12 - 10 = 2$ ✓
- D) $(11, 1)$: $\tfrac{6}{5}(11) - 10 = \tfrac{16}{5} \neq 1$ ✗

Answer: $\boxed{(10,\, 2)}$

## Question 27
**Answer: 4176**

> [!tip] Topic & Method
> **Topic:** Multi-Step Word Problem — Area and Proportional Reasoning
> **Method:** Set up variables for the side lengths using the given ratio, use the total flux to find the side length, then compute the flux through the larger square alone.

Let the smaller square's side $= s$, so the larger square's side $= 3s$.

**Total area of both squares:**
$$s^2 + (3s)^2 = s^2 + 9s^2 = 10s^2$$

**Total flux** = (field strength) × (total area):
$$29 \times 10s^2 = 4{,}640$$
$$290s^2 = 4{,}640$$
$$s^2 = 16 \implies s = 4$$

**Larger square side** $= 3s = 12$ inches, area $= 12^2 = 144$ m²

**Flux through the larger square:**
$$29 \times 144 = \boxed{4{,}176} \text{ volts} \cdot \text{meters}$$
